# Performance Review Process

## Review Cycle

### Annual Performance Reviews
- Conducted once per year in Q4 (October-November)
- Review period covers the full calendar year (January-December)
- All employees participate, including those hired mid-year

### Mid-Year Check-Ins
- Informal progress review conducted in Q2 (June-July)
- Opportunity to adjust goals and provide feedback
- Not tied to compensation changes

## Review Components

### Self-Assessment
- Employee completes self-evaluation form
- Reflect on accomplishments and challenges
- Assess progress toward annual goals
- Due 2 weeks before review meeting

### Manager Evaluation
- Manager completes evaluation based on:
  - Goal achievement
  - Core competencies
  - Contribution to team and company
  - Growth and development

### Peer Feedback (Optional)
- For senior roles or cross-functional positions
- 3-5 peers provide structured feedback
- Focuses on collaboration and impact

## Performance Ratings

### Rating Scale
- **Exceeds Expectations (5)**: Consistently delivers exceptional results, significant impact
- **Above Expectations (4)**: Regularly exceeds goals, strong performance
- **Meets Expectations (3)**: Successfully fulfills role requirements, solid performance
- **Partially Meets Expectations (2)**: Some goals met, but improvement needed
- **Does Not Meet Expectations (1)**: Significant performance issues, immediate action required

### Rating Distribution
- Ratings should follow a normal distribution across the team
- Not everyone can receive the highest rating
- Calibration sessions ensure consistency

## Goal Setting

### SMART Goals
- Specific: Clear and unambiguous
- Measurable: Quantifiable outcomes
- Achievable: Realistic given resources
- Relevant: Aligned with team and company objectives
- Time-bound: Defined timeframe

### Typical Goal Categories
- Performance goals (3-5 objectives)
- Development goals (1-2 learning objectives)
- Behavioral/cultural goals (1 objective)

## Review Meeting

### Preparation
- Manager schedules 60-90 minute meeting
- Both parties review self-assessment and evaluation
- Prepare examples and supporting evidence

### Meeting Structure
1. Employee shares self-assessment (15 minutes)
2. Manager provides feedback and rating (30 minutes)
3. Discussion of achievements and areas for growth (20 minutes)
4. Goal setting for next year (20 minutes)
5. Questions and next steps (10 minutes)

### After the Meeting
- Manager documents discussion in HR system
- Employee and manager both sign review form
- Copy provided to employee within 3 business days

## Compensation Review

### Merit Increases
- Based on performance rating and budget allocation
- Typically 0-8% depending on rating and position
- Effective January 1st following annual review

### Promotion Considerations
- Discussed during review process
- Must demonstrate performance at next level for 6+ months
- Subject to available positions and budget

## Development Planning

### Individual Development Plan (IDP)
- Created or updated during review process
- Identifies skills to develop
- Includes specific actions and timeline
- Resources: training, mentoring, stretch assignments

## Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

### When Required
- Rating of "Does Not Meet Expectations"
- Significant performance concerns mid-year

### PIP Structure
- 60-90 day improvement period
- Specific, measurable objectives
- Weekly check-ins with manager
- HR involvement and documentation
- Possible outcomes: successful completion, role change, or termination
